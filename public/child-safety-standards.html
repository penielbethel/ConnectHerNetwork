Safety Standards Against Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE)

Last updated: 2025-10-30

ConnectHer strictly prohibits any content, activity, or behavior involving child sexual abuse, exploitation, grooming, sexualization of minors, or the depiction/distribution of child sexual abuse material (CSAM). We enforce a zero‑tolerance policy in alignment with global child safety standards and applicable laws.

Prohibited Content and Conduct

  • Creation, possession, sharing, or solicitation of CSAM or exploitative imagery involving minors.
  • Sexualization, grooming, enticement, trafficking, or any exploitation of minors.
  • Links to, instructions for, or tools that facilitate CSAE or access to CSAM.
  • Threats, coercion, or blackmail targeting minors, including sextortion.

Detection and Enforcement

  • We use technical and manual review measures to detect and remove CSAE content.
  • Violations lead to immediate removal of content and permanent account suspension.
  • We preserve relevant data, consistent with law, for reporting to authorities.

Reporting and Escalation

If you encounter content or behavior that may involve CSAE, report it immediately in‑app or via email. We prioritize child safety reports and aim to respond rapidly.

  • In‑app: Use the report function on profiles, posts, or messages.
  • Email: support@connecther.network

Law Enforcement Cooperation

We cooperate with law enforcement and relevant child protection agencies in accordance with applicable laws. We may report credible CSAE incidents and provide required information to authorities.

Minors’ Protection

  • ConnectHer is not intended for children under the age of 13.
  • We remove accounts believed to be owned or controlled by minors under 13.
  • For users aged 13–17, we apply heightened safeguards and content restrictions where applicable.

Contact Information

For urgent safety concerns or to contact our Trust & Safety team:
Email: support@connecther.network

If a child is in immediate danger, contact your local law enforcement first.
